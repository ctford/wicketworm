# Brisbane Test - 2nd Ashes Test 2025-26

**Venue:** The Gabba, Brisbane  
**Dates:** December 4-7, 2025 (Day/Night Test)  
**Result:** Australia won by 8 wickets  
**Series:** Australia leads 2-0

## Scorecard

### England 1st Innings: 334 (74.5 overs)
- **Joe Root 138*** (first Ashes century in Australia)
- Zak Crawley 76
- Jofra Archer 38
- **Mitchell Starc 6/75**
- Brendan Doggett 1/81

### Australia 1st Innings: 511 (145 overs)
- Mitchell Starc 77
- Jake Weatherald 72

- Alex Carey 46
- **All 11 batters reached double figures** (3rd time in Australian Test history)
- **Brydon Carse 4/152**

### England 2nd Innings: 241 (86.3 overs)
- Ben Stokes 50 (152 balls)
- Will Jacks 34
- **Michael Neser 5/42**

### Australia 2nd Innings: 69/2 (10 overs)
- Travis Head 33
- Steve Smith 23* (9 balls)

## Match Summary

Australia secured a comprehensive 2-0 series lead with an 8-wicket victory at the Gabba. Despite Joe Root's magnificent unbeaten 138 and England's improved first-innings total of 334, Australia's batting depth proved decisive. Mitchell Starc and Michael Neser dominated with the ball, and Australia chased down a modest target of 65 with ease.

## Day 1 - Thursday, December 4, 2025

**Evening Session (Day/Night Test):**
England won the toss and elected to bat under lights at the Gabba. Zak Crawley (76) got England off to a solid start, but wickets fell regularly. Joe Root came to the crease and began constructing a masterful innings. He anchored the innings with technical perfection, showcasing why he's one of the modern greats.

By stumps, Root was unbeaten on 135, partnered by Jofra Archer (32*), with England at 325/9 after 74 overs. Root's innings silenced critics who questioned his record in Australia.

**Close of Play:** England 325/9 (Root 135*, Archer 32*)

## Day 2 - Friday, December 5, 2025

**Morning Session:**
England added just 9 runs before Jofra Archer (38) flicked Brendan Doggett to deep backward square leg. Root finished unbeaten on 138*, his first Test century in Australia. England were dismissed for 334.

**Afternoon/Evening Sessions:**
Australia's reply started shakily but gained momentum through the middle order. Jake Weatherald (72), Marnus Labuschagne (65), and Steve Smith (56) all contributed half-centuries in a team batting display. Mitchell Starc (77) provided late fireworks. 

By stumps, Australia had reached 378/6, taking control with a 44-run lead. Alex Carey was unbeaten on 46 with Michael Neser on 15.

**Close of Play:** Australia 378/6 (Carey 46*, Neser 15*) - lead by 44 runs

## Day 3 - Saturday, December 6, 2025

**Day Session:**
Australia's tail wagged effectively, with the hosts eventually declaring or getting bowled out for 511, a massive 177-run first-innings lead. Remarkably, all eleven Australian batters reached double figures - only the third time in Australian Test history.

**Afternoon/Evening Sessions:**
Facing a huge deficit, England crumbled under pressure. The visitors were reduced to 134/6 by stumps, still trailing by 43 runs. Only Ben Stokes (4*) and Will Jacks (4*) remained to provide any resistance.

**Close of Play:** England 134/6 (Stokes 4*, Jacks 4*) - still trailing by 43 runs

## Day 4 - Sunday, December 7, 2025

**Morning/Day Session:**
England's hopes rested on captain Ben Stokes and Will Jacks. The pair produced a gutsy 96-run partnership lasting almost 37 overs, showing the patience and discipline that had been missing from England's batting all series. Stokes battled to 50 off 152 balls.

However, Michael Neser, controversially selected ahead of spinner Nathan Lyon, broke the partnership and triggered another collapse. Neser finished with 5/42 as England were dismissed for 241, setting Australia a target of just 65.

**Afternoon Session:**
Needing only 65 runs for victory, Travis Head (33) came out blazing as Australia raced to 33/0 after five overs. Despite losing two wickets, Steve Smith finished unbeaten on 23 off just 9 balls to seal an emphatic 8-wicket victory in 10 overs.

**Result:** Australia won by 8 wickets

## Player of the Match

**Mitchell Starc** - 6/75, 77 runs, 2/64 (18 wickets in first two Tests)

## Key Statistics

- **Joe Root:** First Test century in Australia (138*) in his 16th Test on Australian soil
- **All-round batting:** Australia became only the 3rd team in their Test history to have all 11 batters score double figures
- **Mitchell Starc:** 18 wickets in first two Ashes Tests, moved to No. 3 among Test bowlers
- **Michael Neser:** Justified selection with 5/42 in England's second innings
- **Ben Stokes:** Resistance innings of 50 off 152 balls - showing rare patience

## Match Turning Points

1. **Australia's batting depth:** All 11 batters contributing showed the strength of Australia's batting lineup
2. **177-run first innings lead:** The massive deficit was too much for England to overcome
3. **Neser's selection:** Picked ahead of Nathan Lyon, Neser delivered with 5/42
4. **Stokes-Jacks partnership:** 96-run stand delayed the inevitable but couldn't change the outcome

## Series Impact

Australia took a commanding 2-0 lead with just three Tests remaining. Despite Root's brilliance and some improved batting displays, England's inability to take 20 wickets or post consistently large totals meant they were outclassed. The series was quickly becoming one-sided, with Australia's pace attack proving devastating and their batting depth overwhelming England's bowling resources.
